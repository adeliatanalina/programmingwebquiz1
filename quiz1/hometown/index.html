Surabaya — City of Heroes

I live and grew up in Surabaya. Surabaya is Indonesia’s second-largest city and the energetic capital of East Java. It’s known as Kota Pahlawan (City of Heroes) for the spirit shown in the 10 November 1945 Battle of Surabaya, honored by the Tugu Pahlawan monument.

About Surabaya

Surabaya is the capital of East Java and one of the busiest cities in Indonesia. People call it the “City of Heroes” because of the Battle of 10 November 1945, when the locals fought hard for independence. You can still feel that spirit at places like Tugu Pahlawan and around the old city areas.

The city is a mix of modern malls, offices, and old neighborhoods. You’ll hear Suroboyoan (the local dialect), see the shark and crocodile symbol (Sura and Baya), and find food everywhere: rawon, lontong balap, tahu tek, and rujak cingur are some favorites. It’s hot most of the time, but evenings are fun with street food, coffee shops, and lights on streets like Tunjungan. Surabaya is also an important port city (Tanjung Perak), so it’s busy with trade, industry, and students from many campuses.

Fun Facts
  • Nickname: Kota Pahlawan—City of Heroes.
  • Emblem: shark (Sura) & crocodile (Baya).
  • Landmarks: Tugu Pahlawan, Sura-Baya Statue, Tunjungan area.
  • Green escapes: Mangrove boardwalks and river promenades.
  • Major hub for education, industry, and shipping.
Hotel Majapahit: Then & Now

Formerly the Oranje/Hotel Yamato, this is where youths tore the Dutch flag’s blue stripe on 19 September 1945, leaving red–white flying. The act of defiance—known as the “Flag Incident”—helped ignite the Battle of Surabaya and the city’s heroic identity.

Today, Hotel Majapahit is a beautifully preserved heritage hotel—quiet gardens, colonial architecture, and a living reminder of Surabaya’s courage.
